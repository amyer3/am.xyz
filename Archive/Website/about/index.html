About Me

Finance is broken. Capital is allocated inefficiently, using processes and techniques developed centuries ago and spreadsheets handed down over the generations. This creates gaps in society; the people who need access to financial resources the most are often left without options and are forced into predatory financing. I believe that technology can fix this.

I've been passionate about finance my entire life. As a toddler, I watched the stock exchange open every morning, at 6 years old I made my first stock pitch (buy EA @ $20), and the second I turned 18, I opened my first brokerage account. A year later I was offered my first job in banking, where I worked for over four years saving up enough money to put myself through school.

I’m motivated to make a real, tangible change for the betterment of society. I want to use my education and abilities to further this transition from slow, inefficient financial systems to precise and technology driven ones. I believe that a faster, smarter and more equal financial system would benefit society, alleviate poverty, and create economic growth in regions where it’s needed the most.
